Control

06 July 2018

When programming, what are some of your triggers? What causes a disproportionate emotional response?

I haven't quite paid attention to what these could be, but in reflection, I know I do tend to get worked up over a few small issues. Firstly, a small thing that has happened multiple times during sprints has been the accidental typo such as forgetting a comma, or spelling a word incorrectly which has let me to go around in circles through my code, chopping and changing to no avail. In my mind I get frustrated and start to think of all the time I am wasting trying to figure this out. When I figure it out, I feel like kicking myself. Almost everytime though, once I've solved it, i turn off the computer and finish for the day, I feel like this is good to get a breather, clear my head, otherwise I could start to make more mistakes in frustration. However, this also gets me as I am on a tight schedule in terms of bootcamp approaching fast. Secondly, when I come across a concept, or block of code I can't seem to wrap my head around it leads me down a path of "can I do this course?, is this right for me?" and so on. With a bit of googling, and help from my sister, it is cleared up, though there are still some concepts that I find confusing, and am hoping in due time I work through these. I have a fear of not being up to everyone elses level, this is not a debilitating fear, though it is always in the back of my mind, and arises in these situations.

How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?

Although not have attempted this exercise myself, I do believe I incorporate most steps in these situations anyway (not knowing it was a technique). I love the idea of the sacred pause as I when I am in these situations i try my best to stop and breathe. Approached I took in the triggers above which has allowed me to find the small typos, however I tend to carry on without reflection and response.
